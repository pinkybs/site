What is laser vision correction?

Laser vision correction is a surgery that improves visual acuity by changing the shape of the cornea (the transparent tissues covering the front of the eyeball) using laser. It is generally known as LASIK (initials of the medical term "laser-assisted in situ keratomileusis").

What is excimer laser?

Excimer laser is a form of ultraviolet laser. In the iLASIK Procedure surgery, it is used for precise remolding of corneal stroma. In an individualized surgery guided by iris registration and wave-front aberration using VISX excimer laser, corneal remolding can provide accurate correction of myopia, hyperopia and astigmatism.

What is femtosecond laser?

Femtosecond laser is a form of infrared laser. It is used to make corneal flap in LASIK surgery. Using IntraLase®, the fifth generation femtosecond laser iFS®, to create corneal flap.

What is Wavefront Guided technology?

Wavefront guided technology measures by comparing the difference between the actual light path and ideal light path passed your eyeball optical system. The precision of WaveScan® wavefront LASIK surgery that identifies and measures individual visual impairment is more accurate than eyeglasses and contact lens. The three-dimensional Wavefront Guided images obtained are transmitted to VISX excimer laser so that you can undergo accurate individualized laser correction surgery.

How does the iLASIK Procedure treat your eyes?

The iLASIK Procedure is a unique combination of IntraLase® and Advanced CustomeVue® technologies. IntraLase® technology creates even and consistent corneal flap using iFS femtosecond laser in LASIK. Then Advanced CustomeVue® technology obtains individual visual impairment in your eyes by WaveScan® Wavefront Guided system, generates three-dimensional Wavefront Guided image, and transmits to VISX excimer laser, so that you can undergo laser vision correction surgery.

Does the iLASIK procedure bring you pain?

Patients can experience pain postoperatively but usually not very serious. The surgery is conducted under anesthesia induced by anesthetic eye drops, and therefore is not so painful. You will feel a little bit of pressure, which lasts less than 1 minute, when corneal flap is being created.

Can the iLASIK Procedure ensure that you achieve the visual acuity of 1.0 after surgery?

Like any other surgery, we cannot guarantee any given result. Surgical efficacy depends on conditions of individual patient, and most patients achieve pre-operative best corrected visual acuity or better after surgery.

What does it mean if your visual acuity is higher than 1.0?

1.0 is the current standard for good visual acuity. However, clinical studies suggest patients undergoing the iLASIK Procedure can achieve a visual acuity above 1.0.

What is the success rate of the iLASIK Procedure? Do you need to wear eyeglasses after surgery?

Success rate varies from center to center. In the Parkway Eye Centre (Singapore), 99% of patients do not need to wear eyeglasses after the iLASIK Procedure.

What will be the result if your eyes move during surgery?

Excimer laser is equipped with three-dimensional eye ball tracker. It can locate and track the pupil, and compensate for slight movement of the eye ball, ensuring that treatment is completed accurately.

Does the iLASIK Procedure have any side effects?

In the first few months after surgery, common transient side effects include dry eye, photo-sensitivity, glare and halo. Most of these resolve without management. It takes 3 – 6 months for visual acuity to completely stabilize after surgery.

Is the iLASIK Procedure approved by the FDA?

Based on clinical studies, the US FDA approves the iLASIK Procedure for treatment of myopia, hyperopia and astigmatism, and confirms that that it is safe and effective.
